Making The Impact

The discussion around women in leadership has intensified over the past few years with concepts such as “breaking the glass ceiling”, women having “a seat at the table” and “leaning in”. However, many of these conversations fail to include and acknowledge the young and disadvantaged women in our society, let alone be invited to have “a seat at the table.” Many such young women and girls from deprived communities are mostly caught up in a vicious cycle of socio-economic hardships, low practical job skills and least of all career opportunities into leadership roles. Yet research over the years have shown that, the economic empowerment of young females and women in general has immense benefits for all in society. Therefore, real socio-economic inclusion for young women in my opinion has to begin at the elementary level and beyond, with a strong emphasis on creating opportunities for the less privileged. Hence, it is for this reason that I developed BRAVE as a way to be part of the conversation and also change the narrative.

BRAVE seeks to proactively connect, inspire, and empower young women from SHS to Tertiary level with women in leadership roles and in higher echelons of power across all industry sectors within our society.

I am of the firm conviction that this model of inclusive leadership and empowerment of young women within our society is the best route to reducing poverty, creating career opportunities, and bridging the divide between the young and the seasoned.

TRACY OWUSU-ADDO, Founder & Chief Executive Lead
